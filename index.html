About

I hold a PhD in Natural Language Processing (Sorbonne Université), defended on 19 June 2026, and I am now a Research Engineer at Arkhn, building LLM-based clinical document generation for a hospital assistant product with compact, self-hosted models. My thesis, Synthetic Data Generation for Clinical Natural Language Processing, studies the privacy–utility trade-off in medical text: producing facsimile clinical documents with large language models (DP fine-tuning, iterative DPO), measuring re-identification risk through linkage and proximity attacks, and validating utility on downstream tasks such as clinical entity extraction and ICD coding. Supervised by Laurent Romary, Éric de la Clergerie and Théo Ryffel.
